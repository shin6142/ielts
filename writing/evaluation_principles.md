# IELTS Writing Evaluation Principles

Based on the official IELTS Writing Band Descriptors (Updated May 2023)

---

## Four Scoring Criteria

### 1. Task Response / Task Achievement (25%)

**What examiners look for:**

| Band | Key Requirements |
|------|------------------|
| 9 | Fully addresses prompt; clear, fully developed position; ideas fully extended and well supported |
| 8 | Sufficiently addresses prompt; well-developed position; ideas well extended and supported |
| 7 | Main parts appropriately addressed; clear and developed position; may over-generalise or lack precision |
| 6 | Main parts addressed; position is relevant but conclusions may be unclear/unjustified; some ideas insufficiently developed |
| 5 | Incompletely addresses prompt; position not always clear; limited ideas, not sufficiently developed |

**Principles:**
- Answer ALL parts of the question directly
- State your position clearly in the introduction
- Develop each main idea with specific examples and explanations
- Avoid over-generalisation - be precise and focused
- Ensure conclusions logically follow from your arguments

---

### 2. Coherence & Cohesion (25%)

**What examiners look for:**

| Band | Key Requirements |
|------|------------------|
| 9 | Message followed effortlessly; cohesion rarely attracts attention; skilful paragraphing |
| 8 | Message followed with ease; logically sequenced; cohesion well managed |
| 7 | Logically organised; clear progression; cohesive devices used flexibly (some inaccuracies OK) |
| 6 | Generally coherent; clear overall progression; cohesion may be faulty/mechanical |
| 5 | Organisation evident but not wholly logical; sentences not fluently linked; repetitive writing |

**Principles:**
- Use paragraphs effectively (one main idea per paragraph)
- Ensure clear progression from introduction → body → conclusion
- Use cohesive devices naturally, NOT mechanically:
  - Reference (it, this, they, these)
  - Substitution (one, do so)
  - Linking words (However, Moreover, Therefore)
- Avoid OVERUSING connectors - this sounds unnatural
- Each paragraph should have a clear central topic

**Common mistakes to avoid:**
- Starting every sentence with "Firstly", "Secondly", "Moreover"
- Repetitive use of the same cohesive device
- No clear topic sentence in paragraphs
- Ideas that don't flow logically

---

### 3. Lexical Resource (25%)

**What examiners look for:**

| Band | Key Requirements |
|------|------------------|
| 9 | Full flexibility; wide range used accurately; sophisticated control; rare spelling errors |
| 8 | Wide resource used fluently/flexibly; skilful use of uncommon/idiomatic items; occasional errors OK |
| 7 | Sufficient flexibility and precision; some less common/idiomatic items; awareness of collocation |
| 6 | Generally adequate; meaning clear despite restricted range; some spelling errors |
| 5 | Limited but minimally adequate; frequent lapses in word choice; noticeable spelling errors |

**Principles:**
- Use topic-specific vocabulary accurately
- Demonstrate awareness of collocations (words that go together)
- Use less common vocabulary when appropriate (don't force it)
- Avoid repetition - use synonyms and paraphrasing
- Spelling errors should NOT impede communication

**Key collocations to master:**
- "play a role" (not "play a part in role")
- "have an impact on" (not "impact on" as verb)
- "take advantage of" (not "take advantages")
- "the number of X is" (singular verb)
- "a large number of X are" (plural verb)

**Risk-taker principle (Band 6):**
> "If the writer is a risk-taker, there will be a wider range of vocabulary used but higher degrees of inaccuracy or inappropriacy."

→ It's better to attempt advanced vocabulary with some errors than to use only simple words perfectly.

### 4. Grammatical Range & Accuracy (25%)

**What examiners look for:**

| Band | Key Requirements |
|------|------------------|
| 9 | Wide range with full flexibility; punctuation/grammar appropriate throughout; rare errors |
| 8 | Wide range flexibly/accurately used; majority error-free; occasional non-systematic errors |
| 7 | Variety of complex structures; generally well controlled; few errors that don't impede communication |
| 6 | Mix of simple and complex; complex structures less accurate; errors rarely impede communication |
| 5 | Limited range, repetitive; complex sentences faulty; frequent errors cause difficulty |

**Principles:**
- Use a VARIETY of sentence structures:
  - Simple sentences
  - Compound sentences (and, but, or, so)
  - Complex sentences (although, because, while, if)
  - Relative clauses (who, which, that)
- Errors in complex structures are more acceptable than only using simple sentences
- Punctuation matters (commas, full stops, semicolons)

**Common grammar errors to eliminate:**

1. **Subject-verb agreement:**
   - "The number of people IS" (singular)
   - "A number of people ARE" (plural)
   - "Dogs ARE popular" (not "Dogs is")

2. **Articles:**
   - "society" (no article for general concept)
   - "the society" (specific society)
   - "a cruel act" (not "cruel act")

3. **Countable vs Uncountable:**
   - "work" (uncountable) - not "works"
   - "information" (uncountable) - not "informations"
   - "advice" (uncountable) - not "advices"

4. **Verb forms after modals:**
   - "can enable" (not "can enables")
   - "should consider" (not "should considers")

5. **Prepositions:**
   - "interested IN"
   - "opposed TO" or "oppose X" (no preposition)
   - "impact X" or "have an impact ON X"

## Task 1 Specific Principles

### Key requirements:
- Present an **overview** of main trends/features
- Select and report **key features**
- Make **comparisons** where relevant
- Use **data** to support descriptions
- Do NOT give opinions

### Structure:
1. **Introduction** - Paraphrase the question (what the graph/chart shows)
2. **Overview** - Main trends/key features (2-3 sentences)
3. **Body 1** - Detailed description with data
4. **Body 2** - Detailed description with data

### Language for data:
- "accounted for" / "made up" / "comprised"
- "increased/decreased significantly/gradually/steadily"
- "peaked at" / "reached a high of"
- "the highest/lowest proportion"
- "approximately" / "around" / "roughly"

## Task 2 Specific Principles

### Key requirements:
- Address ALL parts of the prompt
- Present a **clear position** throughout
- **Extend and support** main ideas
- Use **relevant examples**

### Common question types:

1. **Discuss both views and give your opinion**
   - Discuss view A
   - Discuss view B
   - Give YOUR clear opinion

2. **Agree or disagree**
   - State your position clearly
   - Support with reasons and examples
   - Maintain consistent position throughout

3. **Advantages and disadvantages**
   - Discuss advantages
   - Discuss disadvantages
   - May need to give opinion on which outweigh

4. **Problem and solution**
   - Explain the problems
   - Propose solutions
   - May discuss causes

### Structure:
1. **Introduction** (2-3 sentences)
   - Paraphrase the topic
   - State your position/thesis

2. **Body Paragraph 1** (5-7 sentences)
   - Topic sentence
   - Explanation
   - Example/Evidence
   - Link back to main argument

3. **Body Paragraph 2** (5-7 sentences)
   - Same structure

4. **Conclusion** (2-3 sentences)
   - Restate position
   - Summarise main points
   - Do NOT introduce new ideas

## Quick Checklist Before Submitting

### Task Response
- [ ] Did I answer ALL parts of the question?
- [ ] Is my position clear?
- [ ] Are my ideas well-developed with examples?

### Coherence & Cohesion
- [ ] Does each paragraph have ONE main idea?
- [ ] Are my ideas logically ordered?
- [ ] Did I use cohesive devices naturally (not mechanically)?

### Lexical Resource
- [ ] Did I use topic-specific vocabulary?
- [ ] Did I avoid repetition?
- [ ] Did I check spelling of difficult words?

### Grammar
- [ ] Did I use a variety of sentence structures?
- [ ] Did I check subject-verb agreement?
- [ ] Did I check article usage?
- [ ] Is my punctuation correct?
