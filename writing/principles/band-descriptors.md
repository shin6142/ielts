# IELTS Writing Band Descriptors

Source: [IDP IELTS](https://ielts.idp.com/results/scores/writing)

---

## Four Assessment Criteria

IELTS Writing is evaluated on **four criteria** across 9 band levels (0-9).

| Criteria | Task 1 | Task 2 |
|----------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Task Achievement | Task Response |
| 2 | Coherence and Cohesion | Coherence and Cohesion |
| 3 | Lexical Resource | Lexical Resource |
| 4 | Grammatical Range and Accuracy | Grammatical Range and Accuracy |

---

## 1. Task Achievement (Task 1) / Task Response (Task 2)

### Task 1: Task Achievement
- How well you address the requirements
- Present key features clearly
- Use appropriate format
- Provide an overview with key trends

### Task 2: Task Response
- How thoroughly you explore the prompt
- Develop a clear, supported position
- Address all parts of the question
- Support ideas with relevant examples

---

## 2. Coherence and Cohesion

Both tasks assess:
- **Logical organization** of ideas
- **Clear progression** throughout the response
- **Appropriate use of linking devices** (not overused)
- **Paragraphing** that supports overall clarity
- Cohesion should be natural, not drawing attention to itself

---

## 3. Lexical Resource

Evaluates:
- **Vocabulary range** - variety of words used
- **Accuracy** - correct word choice and spelling
- **Appropriacy** - suitable for academic context
- Higher bands: sophisticated word choice and idiomatic usage
- Minimal spelling errors

---

## 4. Grammatical Range and Accuracy

Measures:
- **Variety of sentence structures** (simple, compound, complex)
- **Accuracy** of grammar and punctuation
- Advanced bands: flexible control with rare errors
- Errors should not impede communication

---

## Band Level Descriptions

### Band 9 - Expert
- Full flexibility and sophisticated control
- Rare minor errors (slips only)
- Natural, effortless expression

### Band 8 - Very Good
- Wide range of structures and vocabulary
- Occasional minor errors
- Skillful use of cohesive devices

### Band 7 - Good
- Good control with occasional lapses
- Clear progression maintained
- Some less common vocabulary used

### Band 6 - Competent
- Adequate but restricted range
- Some inaccuracies that don't obstruct meaning
- Basic cohesive devices used

### Band 5 - Modest
- Limited range of vocabulary and structures
- Errors may cause some difficulty
- Repetition and basic linking only

### Band 4 - Limited
- Basic vocabulary and sentence forms
- Frequent errors impede communication
- Little awareness of style

### Bands 1-3 - Extremely Limited to Non-User
- Minimal control of language
- Meaning often unclear or absent
- Isolated words or phrases only

### Band 0
- No attempt
- Non-English response
- Fully memorized content

---

## Key Differences Between Band Levels

| Aspect | Band 6 | Band 7 | Band 8 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| **Task** | Addresses task, some parts underdeveloped | Addresses all parts clearly | Fully addresses all parts |
| **Coherence** | Arranges ideas coherently | Logically organizes with clear progression | Sequences ideas skillfully |
| **Vocabulary** | Adequate range, some errors | Good range, occasional errors | Wide range, rare errors |
| **Grammar** | Mix of simple/complex, some errors | Variety of structures, few errors | Wide range, rare minor errors |

---

## Tips for Each Criterion

### To Improve Task Achievement/Response:
- Read the question carefully - identify ALL parts
- Write a clear thesis/overview
- Develop each point with examples
- Stay on topic throughout

### To Improve Coherence and Cohesion:
- Use paragraphs effectively (one main idea per paragraph)
- Vary your linking words (not just "firstly, secondly")
- Use referencing (this, these, such) to connect ideas
- Ensure logical flow between sentences

### To Improve Lexical Resource:
- Learn topic-specific vocabulary
- Practice collocations (words that go together)
- Avoid repetition - use synonyms
- Check spelling of difficult words

### To Improve Grammar:
- Use a mix of simple and complex sentences
- Practice relative clauses (who, which, that)
- Master conditionals and passive voice
- Check subject-verb agreement
