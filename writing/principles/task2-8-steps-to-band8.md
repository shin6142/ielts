# 8 Steps to Achieve Band 8 in IELTS Writing Task 2

Source: [IDP IELTS](https://ielts.idp.com/prepare/article-ielts-writing-task-2-8-steps-to-band-8)

---

## Step 1: Answer is Relevant to the Question

- Ensure your essay **directly addresses** what is asked
- Avoid recycled responses or over-generalization
- Use **specific examples and ideas** that relate to the topic
- Don't rely on vague research statistics

---

## Step 2: Answer All Parts of the Question

- Carefully identify how many components exist in the prompt (1, 2, or 3 parts)
- You **must address each part** to achieve Band 6+
- Present a **clear position throughout** the essay
- Write **minimum 250 words**

---

## Step 3: Organize Logically with Clear Progression

- Start with an introduction and move through to a conclusion
- Use **varied linking phrases** rather than repetitive basic connectors
- Employ **referencing and substitution** to avoid repetition

**Examples of varied linking:**
- Instead of: "Firstly... Secondly... Thirdly..."
- Try: "To begin with... Furthermore... In addition to this..."

---

## Step 4: Organize Essays into Paragraphs

Use the **PEEL** structure for body paragraphs:

| Letter | Meaning | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| **P** | Point | Topic sentence introducing the main idea |
| **E** | Example | Supporting evidence or example |
| **E** | Explain | Why this supports your point |
| **L** | Link | Transition to the next paragraph |

**Recommended structure:**
- 4-5 paragraphs depending on question type
- Each paragraph develops a **single idea**

---

## Step 5: Use Uncommon Vocabulary Spelled Correctly

- Select **precise, topic-specific vocabulary**
- Demonstrate sophisticated language use
- Employ **collocation** (words that naturally go together)
- Use **phrasal verbs** accurately
- Avoid spelling errors and typos
- Avoid outdated language

**Examples:**
- Instead of "very important" → "crucial" / "paramount"
- Instead of "a lot of" → "numerous" / "a significant number of"

---

## Step 6: Don't Use Memorized Language

**Avoid:**
- Clichés: "the grass is always greener", "at the end of the day"
- Vague terms: "stuff", "thing", "nowadays", "in today's society"
- Overused phrases that sound unnatural

**Instead:**
- Use clear, specific vocabulary
- Write naturally and appropriately for academic context

---

## Step 7: Use Varied Complex Sentence Structures

Demonstrate mastery of:
- **Relative clauses** ("which", "who", "that")
- **Conditionals** (If... then...)
- **Passive voice** (where appropriate)
- **Complex and compound sentences**

**Key points:**
- Balance complex and simple sentences
- Maintain accuracy in punctuation
- Correct use of articles (a, an, the)
- Ensure subject-verb agreement

---

## Step 8: Use a Comprehensive Checklist

Before submitting, review:

- [ ] **Task Response:** Did I answer all parts of the question?
- [ ] **Coherence:** Is my essay logically organized?
- [ ] **Vocabulary:** Did I use varied and appropriate words?
- [ ] **Grammar:** Are my sentences accurate and varied?
- [ ] **Word count:** Did I write at least 250 words?
