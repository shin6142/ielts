## General Academic Words

| Word | Synonyms | Example Sentence |
|------|----------|------------------|
| significant | considerable, substantial, notable | The facility underwent **significant** changes over the years. |
| increase | rise, grow, expand, surge | The parking area **increased** in size. |
| decrease | decline, drop, reduce, diminish | The number of trees **decreased** significantly. |
| replace | substitute, swap, exchange | The meeting room was **replaced** by a workout room. |
| convert | transform, change, turn into | The space was **converted** into changing rooms. |
| remain | stay, continue, persist | The basketball courts **remained** unchanged. |
| relocate | move, transfer, shift | The reception was **relocated** to the entrance. |
| expand | enlarge, extend, widen | The cafe **expanded** to accommodate more seating. |
| remove | eliminate, take away, get rid of | The trees were **removed** to make space. |
| renovate | refurbish, modernize, upgrade | The building was **renovated** in 2020. |

## Task 2 - Opinion & Argument

### Verbs for Expressing Views

| Word | Synonyms | Example Sentence |
|------|----------|------------------|
| claim | assert, state, maintain, contend | Some people **claim** that meat is unhealthy. |
| believe | think, consider, hold the view, feel | Many **believe** that a vegetarian diet is healthier. |
| think | consider, suppose, reckon, assume | I **think** this argument is flawed. |
| argue | contend, assert, maintain, reason | Experts **argue** that moderation is key. |
| suggest | propose, imply, indicate, recommend | Research **suggests** a link between diet and health. |
| insist | maintain, assert firmly, emphasize | Some **insist** that meat is essential for nutrition. |
| acknowledge | admit, recognize, accept, concede | Critics **acknowledge** the environmental concerns. |
| deny | reject, refute, dispute, contradict | Some **deny** that meat consumption harms the planet. |

### Expressing Agreement/Disagreement

| Phrase | Usage | Example |
|--------|-------|---------|
| I agree that... | full agreement | **I agree that** reducing meat intake has benefits. |
| I disagree with... | full disagreement | **I disagree with** the view that meat is unhealthy. |
| I partially agree... | partial agreement | **I partially agree** that meat has environmental impacts. |
| While I accept that... | concession | **While I accept that** meat production affects the environment, I believe... |
| It is widely believed... | general opinion | **It is widely believed** that plant-based diets are healthier. |
| Some people hold the view... | presenting others' opinions | **Some people hold the view** that eating meat is unethical. |

## Linking Words & Phrases

| Word/Phrase | Usage | Example Sentence |
|-------------|-------|------------------|
| Furthermore | adding information | **Furthermore**, the reception was relocated. |
| However | contrast | **However**, one court was converted to futsal. |
| As a result | showing consequence | **As a result**, the cafe became more spacious. |
| In addition | adding information | **In addition**, two meeting rooms were changed. |
| On the other hand | contrast | **On the other hand**, some areas remained the same. |
| Although | concession | **Although** there were trees, they were removed. |

## Words to Avoid → Better Alternatives

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| got rid of | removed, eliminated |
| became double | doubled in size |
| a lot of | numerous, considerable, substantial |
| very important | crucial, essential, vital |
| things | aspects, factors, elements |
| good | beneficial, advantageous, positive |
| bad | detrimental, harmful, negative |
| big | substantial, significant, considerable |
| small | minor, slight, minimal |
